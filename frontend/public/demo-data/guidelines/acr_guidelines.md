# ACR Guidelines - CT Head Imaging

## Indications

CT head imaging is indicated for:

- Acute neurological symptoms
- Head trauma
- Suspected stroke
- Headache with red flags

## Protocol

### Standard CT Head Protocol

- Slice thickness: 5mm
- Reconstruction: Axial and coronal
- Contrast: Non-contrast for initial evaluation

### CT Head with Contrast

- Indicated for: Suspected mass, infection, or vascular abnormality
- Contrast dose: 100mL non-ionic contrast
- Timing: 60-second delay

## Reporting Standards

### Required Elements

1. Indication for exam
2. Technique description
3. Systematic review of:
   - Brain parenchyma
   - Ventricles
   - Extra-axial spaces
   - Skull and soft tissues
4. Impression with key findings

### Common Findings

- Acute infarct: Describe location, size, and vascular territory
- Mass: Describe location, size, enhancement pattern
- Hemorrhage: Describe location and extent

## Follow-up Recommendations

- Acute infarct: Follow-up MRI recommended
- Mass: Contrast-enhanced MRI recommended
- Stable chronic changes: No follow-up needed
